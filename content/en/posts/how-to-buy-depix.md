Buying DePix is simpler than you might expect. If you have a Brazilian bank account with Pix and a smartphone, you already have everything you need to convert reais into a private stablecoin on the Liquid Network. This guide walks you through the entire process — from signing up to receiving tokens in your wallet.

If you are new to DePix, start with our article on [What is DePix?](/en/posts/what-is-depix/) to understand the basics before making your first purchase.

## What you need before buying DePix

Have these three things ready:

1. **A bank account with Pix** — any Brazilian bank or fintech works (Nubank, Inter, Itau, Bradesco, etc.).
2. **A Liquid wallet** — the DePix App is non-custodial, so you need an external wallet to receive your tokens. [SideSwap](https://sideswap.io/)² is the recommended option and works on mobile.
3. **An email address** — that is all the DePix App requires to sign up. No documents, no selfies, no KYC.

This simplicity is intentional. The DePix App is designed so that anyone can buy a BRL stablecoin with Pix without needing a traditional exchange.

## Step by step: how to buy DePix with Pix

Follow these steps to make your first purchase:

### 1. Create your DePix App account

Go to [depixapp.com](https://depixapp.com/) and sign up using just your email. You can choose between **individual** mode or **merchant** mode, depending on your use case.

### 2. Set up your Liquid wallet

If you do not have a Liquid wallet yet, download [SideSwap](https://sideswap.io/) on your phone. Copy your Liquid address — this is where your DePix tokens will be sent. Keep your recovery words (seed phrase) safe. Whoever controls the seed, controls the money.

### 3. Choose your deposit amount

In the DePix App, enter how much you want to deposit and paste your Liquid wallet address. The minimum is **R$ 5** (about $1) and the maximum is **R$ 6,000/day** per CPF (Brazilian tax ID).

### 4. Pay the Pix QR code

The app generates a Pix QR code. Open your banking app, scan the code, and confirm the payment. It is the same process you already use for any Pix payment.

### 5. Wait for processing

Your DePix tokens will be sent to your Liquid wallet within **24 hours (D+1)**. This window exists to protect you against fraud — the DePix App uses this time to verify that the transaction is legitimate before releasing the tokens. For more details, read about [how DePix protects against scams](/en/posts/how-depix-protects-against-scams/).

### 6. Confirm receipt

Once processing is complete, open your SideSwap wallet and check your DePix balance. That is it — your reais are now on the Liquid Network, under your full control.

## Is DePix safe and trustworthy?

This is the most common question for newcomers, and it is a fair one. Here is what makes DePix secure:

- **Non-custodial**: the DePix App does not hold your funds. Your tokens live in your Liquid wallet, which only you control. Nobody can freeze or seize your money.
- **Anti-fraud verification**: the 24-hour processing window protects against Pix scams. Each transaction is verified before tokens are released.
- **Liquid Network**: operated by a federation of companies with no single point of failure.³ Transactions use Confidential Transactions, which hide the amounts transferred.
- **Minimal data collection**: sign-up requires only an email. Your personal information stays protected.

For a deeper dive into security, read our article on [how DePix protects against scams](/en/posts/how-depix-protects-against-scams/).

## DePix vs Drex: what is the difference?

With Brazil launching Drex (the central bank digital currency), many people wonder how it compares to DePix. The answer comes down to privacy.

**Drex** is a CBDC with full transaction surveillance.⁴ Every payment is recorded and visible to the central bank and financial institutions. It is Pix with better technology but the same monitoring.

**DePix** is the opposite: a stablecoin on the Liquid Network with Confidential Transactions. Amounts are hidden by default, and you hold your own tokens in self-custody. No bank or government has access to your balance.

If financial privacy matters to you, the choice is clear. For a deeper comparison between the banking system and the Liquid Network, read our article on [DePix vs traditional Pix](/en/posts/depix-vs-traditional-pix/).
