## How it is possible to pay via Pix without a bank

To understand how this works, it helps to separate two concepts. Pix is an instant payment system operated by Brazil's Central Bank. Normally, to send a Pix payment, you need a bank account connected to the system. But the DePix App acts as an intermediary: it receives your DePix tokens (a Brazilian real stablecoin on the Liquid Network) and, in return, sends a Pix payment to the recipient on your behalf.

In practice, here is what happens: you have DePix in your digital wallet. When you need to make a payment, you open the DePix App, enter the amount and the recipient's Pix key. The DePix App generates a Liquid Network address for you to send your DePix to. Once the DePix App receives the tokens, it processes the payment and sends the Pix to the recipient's bank account.

The recipient receives a standard Pix payment in their bank account. They do not need to know anything about cryptocurrency, the Liquid Network, or DePix. From their perspective, it is simply a Pix payment like any other.

This means that if you have DePix — regardless of how you obtained them — you can make Pix payments to any person, business, or service that accepts Pix in Brazil. And you do not need a bank account to do it.

## Step by step: how to make a Pix payment with DePix

The process is straightforward and can be completed in just a few minutes. Here is how it works:

**Step 1: Open the DePix App and select "Withdrawal."** Go to [depixapp.com](https://depixapp.com/) in your browser. On the main screen, choose the withdrawal option. This is the feature that converts your DePix into a Pix payment.

**Step 2: Enter the amount in reais.** Type in the value you want to send via Pix. The DePix App will display the applicable fees transparently before you confirm, so there are no surprises.

**Step 3: Enter the recipient's Pix key.** Input the Pix key of the person or business you want to pay. The key can be a CPF (Brazilian tax ID), email address, phone number, or random key — any Pix key format works.

**Step 4: The DePix App generates a Liquid address.** After confirming the details, the DePix App displays a Liquid Network address. This is the address where you need to send your DePix.

**Step 5: Send your DePix from SideSwap to the generated address.** Open the SideSwap app (or any other Liquid wallet you use), paste the address provided by the DePix App, and send the corresponding amount of DePix.

**Step 6: The DePix App processes and sends the Pix.** Once the DePix tokens are received on the Liquid Network, the DePix App processes the transaction and sends the value via Pix to the specified key. The recipient receives the money in their bank account.

The entire process takes just a few minutes. The recipient gets a normal Pix payment, with no indication that it originated from a cryptocurrency transaction.

## The difference between Pix and DePix in this context

A common question arises: if the DePix App sends a Pix payment at the end, what is the difference compared to a regular Pix? The difference lies in the source of funds and the relationship with the banking system.

With a conventional Pix payment, the money comes from your bank account. The bank records the transaction, knows who you sent it to, how much you sent, and when. This information is stored on the bank's servers and in the Central Bank's system.

When you use the DePix App to make a withdrawal, the money comes from your DePix — digital assets that exist on the Liquid Network, outside the banking system. The recipient's bank sees an incoming Pix payment, but has no visibility into the origin of the funds on the blockchain. For a detailed comparison, check out our article on [DePix vs. traditional Pix](/en/posts/depix-vs-traditional-pix/).

This is not about avoiding legal obligations. It is about exercising your right to move money without having every transaction monitored and recorded by multiple financial institutions.
