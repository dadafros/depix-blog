Every time you buy something with a bank transfer or credit card, that transaction is permanently recorded. Your bank knows which stores you shop at, how much you spend, and when. Credit agencies build profiles from this data. For most of history, cash provided a natural layer of privacy for everyday purchases. In the digital world, gift cards and prepaid cards purchased with cryptocurrency bring that privacy back.

In this article, we will show you how to use DePix¹ to buy gift cards and prepaid cards privately, without going through identity verification (KYC) processes.

## Why gift cards protect your privacy

When you pay with a bank transfer or credit card, the transaction is recorded by your bank, the merchant's bank, and often by central authorities. The merchant name, the amount, and the date live permanently in your financial history. This data is used to build consumer profiles, evaluate creditworthiness, and in some cases, shared with third parties.

Gift cards break that chain. When you buy a gift card with cryptocurrency, the gift card platform receives crypto and delivers a redemption code. When you use that code at a store like Amazon, Netflix, or Uber, the store sees only the gift card. There is no link between the gift card and your bank account. No statement entry, no data point for profiling.

This is not about evading taxes or hiding illegal activity. It is the digital equivalent of paying cash at a physical store. Just as no one questions you for buying coffee with paper bills, buying a gift card with cryptocurrency is simply a choice to keep your finances private.

## Platforms for buying gift cards with crypto

Several platforms allow you to purchase gift cards using cryptocurrency without requiring identity verification. Here are the most established options:

**Bitrefill**² is the largest and most comprehensive. It offers over 5,000 gift cards from brands like Amazon, Netflix, Uber, Spotify, Apple, Google Play, and hundreds more across dozens of countries. It accepts Bitcoin via the Lightning Network, along with other cryptocurrencies. No KYC is required for purchases within reasonable limits (up to $5,000 per day). Delivery is instant when paying via Lightning.

**CoinCards** serves primarily the United States, Canada, and Europe, offering gift cards from hundreds of retailers including Walmart, Best Buy, and Airbnb. It accepts Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies without requiring KYC for most purchases.

**Coinsbee**³ is another solid option, with over 5,000 brands across 185 countries. It accepts more than 200 cryptocurrencies, including USDT. No KYC is required for transactions below EUR 1,000 per purchase (up to EUR 10,000 total). You only need an email address to receive your voucher.

**CryptoRefills** lets you spend Bitcoin, USDT, USDC, ETH, and over 100 other cryptocurrencies on gift cards from more than 5,000 brands, also without KYC.

All of these platforms work similarly: you choose the gift card, pay with crypto, and receive the code by email or directly on screen. The process takes just a few minutes.

This path is more direct if the gift card platform accepts USDt. Coinsbee, for example, accepts USDT across multiple networks.

## Prepaid virtual cards: a growing alternative

Beyond gift cards, there are prepaid virtual cards that can be loaded with cryptocurrency. Unlike gift cards, which work at specific stores, a prepaid virtual card functions like a Visa or Mastercard and can be used at any online merchant.

Some options offering cards without KYC or with minimal verification include:

**SolCard** offers a prepaid card that can be added to Apple Pay or Google Pay without requiring KYC for basic use. The balance is loaded with crypto.

**Laso Finance** is a platform that issues stablecoin-funded prepaid cards without requiring identity verification for virtual card issuance.

**Bing Card** offers virtual cards that can be loaded with cryptocurrency without KYC for basic usage.

It is important to be transparent about the limitations. Most of these cards work best for occasional purchases and smaller amounts. Because they operate on the Visa and Mastercard networks, which function in regulated environments, platforms that do not require KYC today may add requirements in the future. Always check current terms before using any service.

## Practical use cases

Combining DePix with gift cards and prepaid cards opens up practical possibilities for everyday life:

**Online shopping without linking your bank account.** When you use an Amazon gift card, the purchase does not appear on your bank statement. For anyone who shares a bank account or simply values discretion, this matters.

**International purchases.** Gift cards from American or European stores can be purchased with crypto regardless of where you are located. This eliminates the need for an international credit card and the excessive exchange rate fees that banks typically charge.

**Mobile top-ups.** Bitrefill offers mobile top-ups for carriers in dozens of countries, including Brazil. You can keep your phone active without linking a credit card.
