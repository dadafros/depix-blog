You have probably heard the saying: "if you are not paying for the product, you are the product."

Brazil's Pix system is free, instant, and available around the clock — and that is genuinely impressive. But free does not mean without cost. Every Pix transaction you make is recorded, stored, and analyzed by your bank and the central bank. Your spending habits, your income, your schedule, who you send money to and how much — all of it becomes data. And data has value. The DePix App is not free. But it respects your privacy and gives you sovereignty over your money. This article provides an honest, practical comparison of both options.

## What Pix does right

Before any comparison, Pix deserves credit for what it has accomplished. Launched by Brazil's central bank in November 2020,² it has fundamentally transformed payments in the country.

Pix is instant, operates 24/7 including holidays, and is free for individuals. You can transfer money using a Pix key, QR code, or bank details, and the funds arrive in seconds. It processes more daily transactions than credit and debit cards combined. By any measure, Pix is a remarkable achievement in financial infrastructure.

The DePix App does not exist to replace Pix. In fact, the DePix App uses Pix as its infrastructure — deposits and withdrawals happen via Pix. The difference lies in what happens between deposit and withdrawal: where and how your money is stored.

With traditional Pix, your money resides in your bank account. The bank is the custodian. The bank can impose fees, enforce limits, freeze your account for administrative reasons, or comply with court orders that block your funds. You trust the bank to hold and release your money when you need it.

With DePix, your digital reais live on the Liquid Network, a decentralized Bitcoin sidechain.³ You control your own addresses and keys. No single institution can freeze or block your DePix. Your money is as accessible as your internet connection.

This does not mean keeping money in a bank is bad. For most people, most of the time, banks work perfectly well. But having the option to keep some of your money outside the banking system is a freedom that did not exist before cryptocurrency. The DePix App makes that option accessible to anyone who uses Pix.

## Transaction privacy

Traditional Pix offers convenience but not privacy. Every Pix transaction is recorded and visible to the sending bank, the receiving bank, and the central bank. These institutions know exactly how much you transferred, to whom, when, and how frequently.

For everyday transactions, most people do not think about this. But if you value financial privacy, it is worth knowing that every centavo you move through Pix is monitored and recorded in multiple databases.

The DePix App operates on the Liquid Network, which features Confidential Transactions by default. This means that the amounts transferred between Liquid addresses are cryptographically hidden from outside observers. Only the parties involved in a transaction know the value.

This is not about hiding anything illegal. It is about the same principle that makes you close the curtains at home — you are not doing anything wrong, but you still value your privacy. For a deep dive into how the DePix App protects your financial privacy, read our article on [financial privacy with DePix](/en/posts/financial-privacy-depix/).

## Limits and restrictions

Banks impose limits on Pix transactions. There are daily limits, reduced nighttime limits (for security), and per-transaction limits. These vary by bank and often require manual adjustment through the app or even a visit to a physical branch.

With the DePix App, the limits are different. There is no artificial nighttime reduction. Pix deposits range from R$5 to R$6,000 per day per CPF,¹ and these values are displayed transparently before any operation. On the Liquid Network itself, you can move your DePix freely at any time, with no time-based or amount restrictions.

Additionally, with traditional Pix, your bank can change your limits without notice or implement new restrictions unilaterally. With DePix on the Liquid Network, the rules are clear and do not change at the discretion of a single entity.

## Cost comparison

Pix is free for individuals in the vast majority of cases. This is one of its greatest advantages and a major reason for its massive adoption.

The DePix App has fees associated with deposits and withdrawals, displayed transparently before each operation. You always know exactly what you will pay before confirming.

If zero cost is your only criterion, traditional Pix wins. But if you value privacy, autonomy, and access to the crypto ecosystem, the cost of using the DePix App may be well worth it — especially for larger amounts where the advantages become more significant relative to the fees.

## Access to the crypto ecosystem

Perhaps the biggest advantage the DePix App has over traditional Pix is the access it provides to the cryptocurrency world. With DePix, you are on the Liquid Network, which is part of the Bitcoin ecosystem.

This means you can easily move value between DePix and Bitcoin, interact with other assets on the Liquid Network, and participate in a global financial ecosystem that does not depend on any single government or central bank.

If you are a remote worker who needs to [convert crypto to reais](/en/posts/how-to-convert-crypto-to-reais/), the DePix App is the perfect bridge between your crypto earnings and your everyday expenses in reais. If you want to start investing in Bitcoin, the DePix App is an accessible entry point — you are already on the same network.

## When to use each option

The reality is that DePix and traditional Pix are not mutually exclusive. You can and should use both, each for what it does best.

Use traditional Pix for everyday payments like groceries, restaurants, and utility bills. Use it for quick transfers between friends and family, and for any situation where zero cost is the top priority.

Use the DePix App when you want to keep reais outside the banking system with more autonomy. Choose it when financial privacy matters to you, when you need to convert between Bitcoin and reais quickly, and when you want to participate in the Liquid Network and Bitcoin ecosystem.
